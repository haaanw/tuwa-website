Write 400-600 words covering:
- Log workouts with exercises, sets, reps, weight, and RPE (rate of perceived exertion)
- Tuwa calculates session load from volume and intensity, then tracks your training load over time using exponentially weighted moving averages
- Acute load (recent 7 days) vs chronic load (28-day trend) -- the ratio between them is your Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio
- When acute load spikes above chronic (ratio > 1.3-1.5), injury risk increases significantly. Tuwa detects these spikes and warns you before you do the session, not after
- Personal record tracking: Tuwa automatically flags when you hit new maxes in weight, reps, or volume -- no manual logging needed
- Reps in reserve (RIR) as an intensity metric alongside RPE

- The ACWR concept comes from sports science research by Tim Gabbett and others -- originally studied in team sports (rugby, cricket), now applied across all training modalities
- EWMA (exponentially weighted moving averages) gives more weight to recent training sessions than older ones, making the model responsive to actual training patterns
- The "sweet spot" is an ACWR between 0.8 and 1.3 -- enough stimulus for adaptation without excessive injury risk
- Include `<AcwrChart />` component after the explanatory text (import from `../../components/charts/AcwrChart.astro`)
- The chart shows how acute load fluctuates around chronic load over a training cycle

Write 500-700 words covering:
- The cold-start problem: any algorithm that relies on personal historical data is useless on day one. Most apps either give you garbage scores or tell you "come back in two weeks"
- Tuwa's approach: start with population-level baselines (validated from sports science literature), then progressively personalize as your data accumulates
- Day 1: wellness check-in gives subjective data immediately. If HealthKit data is available, Tuwa uses it but flags that baselines are still being established

- Day 7+: personal baselines stabilize. Tuwa transitions from population-weighted to individual-weighted scoring
- Tuwa is honest about this: the UI explicitly tells you when scores are based on population data vs your personal baseline. No false precision.

- False precision is worse than no data -- if an app says "your recovery is 85" on day one with zero physiological data, that number means nothing
- Athletes who train by feel already have good instincts. Tuwa's cold-start respects that -- it supplements your judgment rather than overriding it with fabricated scores
- The transition from population-to-personal baselines is smooth and transparent. You see your confidence intervals narrow as data accumulates.
- No chart on this page -- the value proposition is trust and honesty, not data visualization
